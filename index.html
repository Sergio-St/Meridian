AI Interpretation
Patients with type 2 diabetes over 50 who took metformin for at least 6 months showed a statistically significant reduction in cardiovascular event rates across all three outcomes.

The largest effect was for myocardial infarction (−39% relative risk). These findings are consistent with the UKPDS trial and 2021–2023 meta-analyses.

A hazard ratio of 0.77 (95% CI 0.68–0.87) means metformin users had a 23% lower risk of a cardiac event compared to non-users.
